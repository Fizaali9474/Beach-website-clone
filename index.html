The Beach Page:

Beach is a narrow strip of land separating a body of water from inland areas. Beaches are usually made of sand, tiny grains of rocks and minerals that have been worn down by constant pounding by wind and waves.

Lake Side:

A sea is a large body of salty water. There are particular seas and the sea. The sea commonly refers to the ocean, the wider body of seawater. Particular seas are either marginal seas, second-order sections of the oceanic sea.

Littoral:

A beach is a landform alongside a body of water which consists of loose particles. The particles composing a beach are typically made from rock, such as sand, gravel, shingle, pebbles, etc., or biological sources, such as mollusc shells or coralline algae.

Big Waves:

A beach is a strip of land that borders an ocean, river or lake. The beach slopes gently downward from the dry backshore to the place where the waves break, the foreshore. Sandy beaches are made chiefly of quartz particles, others are rocky, while some are frozen.
